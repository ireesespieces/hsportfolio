About Me

Ever since I got my first laptop in 3rd grade, I became very interested in the world of computers and what it has to offer. I loved to explore what the websites had to offer. While other people would only use the features that others taught, I would explore all the features the site had to offer. I really started to get into computer hardware when my dad built a computer in 2020. A year later, I was inspired to also build my own computer. At Glen A. Wilson High School, I’ve taken the AP Computer Sciences and Cybersecurity, as well as Intro to Engineering Design. At Rio Hondo, I have taken Intro to Digital Photography and at Cerritos College I will be taking Intro to Computer Information Systems. This led me to want to major in Computer Engineering. I’ve also started a few projects, such as my right-hand indicator and 16 key macropad. The right-hand indicator was made for my friend, who got into a motorcycle accident and paralyzed his left arm. This way, he would be able to safely hit the turn signal instead of reaching his whole hand over the steering wheel. In the future, I hope to go to a 4 year college and also get a Masters in Computer Engineering.

Reflection

These past few years at Wilson, I’ve learned about programming, cybersecurity, and engineering. From coming in as a freshman having very basic programming to being a junior and learning 3 programming languages have been extremely helpful for me and my problem solving skills. I’ve experienced tremendous growth both personally and academically. Whether it was competing at SkillsUSA or Cyber competitions or developing my creative skills in CAD and video editing, I’ve become much more confident in my ability to take initiative and adapt quickly. I have two favorite memories. First, I competed at SkillsUSA Nationals in Georgia, being one of the top people in IT Services has shown me how much potential I had, but also how much potential still lies ahead. Second was when I was able to go to the FBI Forensic Lab in freshman year. That opened my eyes in letting me understand that high school was able to open a lot of new opportunities.

Looking forward, I’m excited to continue pushing my limits in both technology and media. I hope to further develop my programming skills, engineering skills, and continue gaining my CompTIA certifications. I’m aiming to get a masters degree in Computer Engineering and I hope to be able to pursue and bring my skills to something related to computer hardware.
